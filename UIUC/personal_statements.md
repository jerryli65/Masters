#1. Please describe pivotal experiences, opportunities, and/or challenges (including any financial or access to education barriers) that have influenced your educational and professional development. (250 words max)

I have found that I am most engaged when the path to an answer is unclear, when understanding the system is half the problem. During my undergraduate degree, I joined a data science research lab utilizing dynamic programming of connected agents to draw conclusions about formal logic applied to imperfect societies. Being forced to use creativity to reason about the means rather than the ends was a pathway to gaining a deeper understanding of the topics taught in school. This led to an appreciation for an array of topics that would be introduced, but not covered, at the undergraduate level.

At work, I have been thrust into a major refactoring of the codebase that includes more modern tooling and workflows. My coworkers expect me to be more familiar than them with these aspects across the product lifecycle, perhaps due to anticipated curriculum updates that would be perfectly on-par with industry trends. This never tends to be the case for any undergraduate degree, but significant outside and additional research and involvement have allowed me to be serviceable in such modern topics. Both the process of piecing together information as well as having a notable impact on the industry are satisfying and leave me craving more.

Both experiences were supplementary to my main role at that time, yet it sparked greater joy to investigate. Depth is the next step to make the most of the breadth introduced by the undergraduate curriculum. I anticipate that this opportunity will come from the Illinois program.
